## Summary
- UCL needs a common language understood by everyone so that concepts are standardised across applications.
- The EDM standardises concepts, definitions, and supports greater application uniformity.
- The EDM provides seed data models for new applications.
- The EDM provides a grand data model as a basis for application integration and data exchange.
- The EDM is also known as the Canonical Data Model.
- The EDM is a visual representation of enterprise data and a system-agnostic common language for integrations.
- Systems often have their own terminology, and data must be transformed into enterprise terminology to support integrations.
- Using the EDM supports agility when changing, upgrading, or replacing source systems because consumer flows do not need to change.
- Data ownership is important to maintain business rules and common understanding.
- The EDM helps move away from different rules for the same entities and improves data quality and consistency.
- Data owner is accountable for data quality and policies/processes governing the data as business information.
- Data steward is responsible for practical day-to-day oversight and sign-off or escalation for data modelling decisions.
- Data custodian ensures the systems can provide the level of management the business needs and often maps physical data to the eventual data model.
- Data modeller defines new entities, attributes, and cardinality when EDM gaps are identified.
- Creating the EDM involves defining the subject area, entities, cardinality, relationships, logical data model, and updating the domain data model in ER studio.
