## Governance and Process Standards
* **Integrated by Design**: Integration capabilities of new applications must be evaluated during the procurement process. Funds must be allocated to enable integration if not natively supported.
* **Lean Governance**: Governance within Domains and Product Teams is based on adherence to these architectural guardrails.
* **Devolved Ownership**: Product teams own the interfaces in and out of their products, including compliance and ongoing service improvement.

## Technical Standards
* **API-First & Event-Driven**: Data exposure from source systems must be agnostic of consuming systems. Business processes should prefer orchestration of events and APIs.
* **Cloud Native Implementation**: Move away from on-premise toolkits to platforms offering high availability and zero manual intervention.
* **Continuous Synchronisation**: Synchronisation between services must be based on event-driven publish/subscribe patterns.
* **Simplified Development**: Prefer UI-driven abstractions and low-code environments over low-level coding for standard integrations.

## Anti-Patterns
* **Point-to-Point (P2P)**: Custom-built, point-to-point integrations are difficult to maintain and impossible to reuse.
* **Tactical Silos**: Solutions requested after service procurement often result in disconnected experiences and data silos.
